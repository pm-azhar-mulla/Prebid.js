Major Floods in Northern Region Displace Thousands, Relief Operations Underway
Heavy rainfall continues for the third consecutive day as authorities struggle to evacuate affected areas

NEW DELHI: Catastrophic flooding has affected over 50,000 people in the northern regions as heavy monsoon rains continue unabated for the third consecutive day. The meteorological department has issued red alerts for several districts as water levels in major rivers continue to rise above the danger mark.

Relief operations are underway with the army and national disaster response teams being deployed to the worst-affected areas. "We are working round the clock to ensure the safety of citizens and provide necessary supplies to those affected," said the Home Minister in a press briefing yesterday.
